You don't remember the last time you heard from your estranged Grandmother who lived on a far away island. Your Mother refused to talk about her so you never brought it up. Therefore you didn't know what to make of it when you received a letter from her with only the words "Come quickly! There isn't much time!" scrawled on it, accompanied with a pre-paid one-way ticket for a boat to the island she lives on. You considered discussing it with your Mother but decided against it.

You didn't have much else going on at the time and money hadn't been coming in. You used to get a small but reasonable amount of business as a mini-celebrity in your local town but people no longer seemed interested, and you yearned for the return of easy income. Sensing opportunity for something new, you packed your bags and left to meet your grandmother and see what the deal was. You told your Mother you were going abroad on holiday with friends.

A couple of days later the boat arrived at the small island. Your first task was simple, find your Grandmother's house. All you had to go on was the return address on the back of the letter's envelope, which was in your bag.

This must be the house. You knock on the front door and a small while later you hear multiple bolts unlock. The door slowly creaks open and your Grandmother is standing there, looking very frail and hunched over a long walking stick made from a gnarled oaken branch.

"Oh good, you came! I haven't seen you since you were a small baby!" she kisses you on the cheek then turns around and hobbles over to her chair. "Make yourself at home but don't delay, you're needed at the Magician Assembly."

"I'm sorry what?" you reply, confused. "The Magician Assembly? What are you talking about? Could you please explain what is going on?"

"Don't tell me your Mother hasn't told you about our family lineage being the Guardians of the Inner Circle! What an abdication of her responsibilities! Oooh your Mother never behaved! We are required to seal off the gateway when Hades tries to return every 30 years! Only our bloodline can do it! I'm too old and frail and your Mother always refused and ran away! This will be the end of us all!"

You stare blankly, trying to make sense of what you have just heard, and then you start to panic. "But... but I don't know anything about this until now! How can I be expected to do this! I don't know what I'm supposed to do! I'm not a magician! My job might be to talk to the dead but it's only an act! I can't actually do it!"

"Rubbish! Of course you can! Pick up that locket on my table over there and put it on."

You pick up Grandma's locket.

Use the icon at the top of the page to go to your equipment and equip the locket. Equippable items you own are displayed on the equipment page instead of your inventory.

"That surge of power you felt when you wore the locket, that's an enchanted item passed down through generations of our family. Only those with an affinity for magic will feel that power."

"OK, but I still don't know how to use magic in any sort of useful way."

"Well then dear, I will just have to show you, now focus your mind."

Use the icon above to visit the skill menu and cast a skill.

"See how easy that was? The magic has been inside you all along, you just didn't know how to apply it."

"Can you teach me more Grandma?"

"I'm afraid I am not the best person to speak to. But if you go to the personal trainer in town I'm sure he will be able to teach you how to utilise your powers in a way that works best for you."

"So what now?"

"Well dear that's all from me. My house is open to you any time you need to rest and recover. I have a spare room you can use. Feel free to stop by and visit any time. In the meantime, you need to go to the Magician Assembly and help seal off the gateway!"

"OK, thank you for your help Grandma. I will try my best."

"Oh, and one last thing, don't forget to eat! You can't do very much in a day on an empty stomach! The better food you eat, the more time you will have to do things in the day. Please take this, I baked it myself."

You get a cookie.

You kiss your Grandma goodbye and then step out off the house, and head towards town. Partway there you stop to think deeply about what just happened. You didn't know what to expect when you came here, but this certainly wasn't what you had in mind.
